WATER CONSERVATION

Water is essential for all forms of life on Earth, covering about 71% of the planet's surface and playing a key role in shaping
ecosystems and supporting biodiversity. Composed of hydrogen and oxygen (Hâ‚‚O), water exists in three states liquid, solid,
and gas allowing it to cycle through oceans, rivers, lakes, and the atmosphere. This cycling, known as the water cycle,
helps regulate the planet's climate and temperature, while also transporting nutrients that sustain plants, animals, and human
communities. Freshwater is a critical, yet limited, resource, with less than 3% of Earth's water being freshwater suitable for
drinking, agriculture, and sanitation. Despite its abundance, clean water is becoming increasingly scarce due to pollution,
climate change, and overuse, underscoring the need for sustainable water management and conservation efforts worldwide.
